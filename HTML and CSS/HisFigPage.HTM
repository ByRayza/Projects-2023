Historical Figure: Genghis Khan

Introduction

Genghis Khan (aka Temujin) was a significant historical figure known for being the first Khagan (aka ruler) of the Mongol Empire, one of the largest contiguous land empires in history.

Early Life

Genghis Khan was born in 1162 in Mongolia. Before he turned 10, an enemy clan poisoned his father, and his clan abandoned him and his family. Eventually, he married Borte in 1178. Temujin later united the Mongols and became Genghis Khan.

Importance

Genghis Khan's contributions to history had a lasting impact on world history. He was the first Khagan (aka ruler) of the Mongol Empire. He conquered vast amounts of land in China and Central Asia. By the time of his death in 1227, the Mongol Empire stretched from the Sea of Japan to the Caspian Sea. His descendants would further expand the empire throughout Asia and into Eastern Europe.
